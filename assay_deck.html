01 · The problem

Nobody keeps score
of judgment.

Reality does — eventually. But its scorecard arrives late, and it's contaminated.

Late: the trader learns he was overconfident after he loses the money. The doctor learns after the misdiagnoses. You pay tuition for the lesson.

Contaminated: a winning P&L mixes skill, luck, position size, and a bull market. Three of those aren't judgment.

And prediction markets are no better
They weight belief by money. Whoever bets most moves the price most — so the market treats the richer forecaster as the more credible one.
02 · The idea

Assay

A clean scorecard for judgment. Not how much you bet — when you say 70%, are you right 70% of the time?

The hard part is cold-start.

Calibration needs a track record. Track records take months. So nobody builds this — and markets fall back on money, because it's the only weight available.

04 · What this is

Not a model. Not a market.
A measurement layer.

It predicts nothing. It has no opinion about where any price goes.
It takes no bets. No odds, no counterparty, no money.
It scores the people who do both — and hands back a number.

GET /reputation/{handle} → { brier, skill, weight, calibration_curve }

One endpoint. Any market — or any team — can call it to decide how much to trust someone.

The same problem is everywhere

Many people have opinions, they are not equally good, and nobody is tracking who is. Markets are just the fastest place to prove it — ground truth arrives in hours, not years.

05 · What we built · what broke · what's next

Brought (infra only)

  • Price ingest · deploy config

Known limitations — stated, not hidden

  • N=9, 10 questions. Scores are directional, not definitive.
  • Absolute weighting is degenerate when nobody beats baseline. A real design flaw, found by running on real people.
  • Softmax won on live, lost on holdout. At this sample size, that's what an honest result looks like.
